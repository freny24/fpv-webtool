# Wednesday Meeting - UI Changes & New Features Explanation

## For Aline (Non-Technical)

### What We Changed - Simple Overview

We made two major improvements to the FPV tool:

---

### **1. User Contribution Feature** 
**What it is:** A way for people around the world to report floating solar sites they know about.

**How it works:**
- User clicks "Contribute a Site" button
- A form pops up asking them to fill in:
  - **Location** (latitude & longitude - like GPS coordinates)
  - **Site name** (what it's called, optional)
  - **Country** (optional)
  - **Source** (where they learned about it: field visit, news, satellite images, local knowledge, etc.)
  - **Notes** (any extra info to help us verify)
- They submit it
- **Our team reviews** the submission before it appears on the map
- **We have duplicate detection** - if someone submits a site very close to one already in our system, we flag it as a potential duplicate but still review it

**Why it matters:** Instead of only showing sites we've found, the map now grows from community input. Real people on the ground can help us discover sites we missed.

**Visual indicator on map:** Community-submitted approved sites show as small **green dots** 🟢, different from the regular FPV sites.

---

### **2. Climate Zones Feature**
**What it is:** Categorizing all floating solar sites by their climate type (using Köppen climate classification system).

**How it works:**
- Each FPV site now has a climate zone label (like "Tropical", "Temperate", "Arid", etc.)
- Users can **search by climate** in the search bar - just type "tropical" or "arid"
- When they search for a climate zone, the map **zooms in and highlights all sites** in that climate
- Each climate zone has a **different color ring** around it on the map

**Why it matters:**
- Helps researchers understand if floating solar works differently in different climates
- Users can study climate patterns (e.g., "Show me all tropical FPV sites")
- Provides geographic and climate insights

**Visual changes:** 
- Marker rings around sites now show different colors based on climate zone (not just one color)
- When you select a climate search result, a banner appears at the top showing which climate is filtered and how many sites
